# GeoContext Editor — Design Context

## Users

Digital humanists working with small-to-medium datasets who want simple
GIS visualisations without learning QGIS. Researchers, archivists,
museum staff, graduate students.

They have a *story* to tell with spatial data — an excavation campaign,
a historical trade route, a narrative-geography essay, a digital
archive launch — not complex spatial analysis. They are comfortable
reading JSON; less comfortable with map-projection arithmetic.

Sessions are publication-driven, not daily. The tool gets opened in
focused bursts when a paper, an exhibition, or an archive page needs
maps.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **scholarly, precise, quiet.**

Voice: a museum wall-label, not a SaaS marketing page. Authoritative
without shouting. The product should feel like the kind of software
you'd find on the laptop of a humanities librarian at a small but
serious institution.

**Anti-tones**: hustle, gamification, "let's go!", "AI-powered ✨".

## Aesthetic Direction

**Quiet modernist — Swiss museum signage.**

- Restrained warm-neutral surfaces (parchment-ivory in light mode,
  late-evening-study in dark mode). Neither mode is the other inverted;
  each gets its own warmth.
- One decisive accent: a sienna / terracotta borrowed from topographic
  plates. It appears only on the *current* selection or for destructive
  moments. Everything else lives in warm neutrals.
- Geometric grotesque typography throughout (single family, heavy
  weight contrast for hierarchy). One narrow monospace for coordinates,
  zooms, and counts — tabular figures so numbers don't jitter.
- Hairline rules and grid alignment instead of cards-in-cards.
- Asymmetric, ratio-driven compositions. Generous gutters; tighten only
  where information density genuinely demands it.

**Type stack**:
- *Switzer* (Indian Type Foundry / Fontshare) — body + display, weight
  contrast handles the hierarchy.
- *JetBrains Mono* — coordinates, zoom levels, JSON view.

**Anti-references**: glossy SaaS dashboards, glassmorphic admin
panels, neon dev-tool aesthetics, generic icon-card grids, gradient
hero numbers.

## Theme

Auto (follow `prefers-color-scheme`). Both modes carry equal craft.

- **Light**: warm ivory paper, deep ink, sienna accent. Reads like a
  printed plate.
- **Dark**: late-evening study, warm-tinted black surfaces, the same
  sienna at slightly lower chroma so it doesn't glow.

## Accessibility

- WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large).
- Honour `prefers-reduced-motion` — transitions collapse to opacity
  fades or instant changes.
- Focus rings are *visible* (no `outline: none` without a replacement)
  and use the accent colour at a deliberate offset.

## Design Principles

1. **Plate over panel.** Prefer hairline rules and grid alignment over
   boxes and cards. The editor should feel like a typeset plate, not a
   stack of widgets.
2. **One decisive accent.** A single warm accent appears only on the
   *current* item or destructive moments. Restraint is the personality.
3. **Numbers know their place.** Coordinates, zooms, and counts use
   tabular-figure monospace. Quiet authority, no jitter on edit.
4. **Both modes are first-class.** Light is parchment + ink; dark is
   late-evening study. Never the same palette inverted.
5. **Whitespace is content.** Generous gutters at full width; tighten
   only where data density demands it. Never crowd to fill space.
